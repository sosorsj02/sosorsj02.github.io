Because rift herald is a potentially game-changing objective in any game, we will look at the impact of rift heralds on the outcome (win or loss) of a game. If you're new to the game or are just here because it seemed interesting, rift herald is an objective in which, when killed, drops an eye and allows the player who picks up the eye to summon the rift herald anywhere on the map. After walking to an enemy objective and starting a short charge up, the rift herald will ram into the nearest enemy turret, inhibitor, or nexus, dealing massive amounts of damage. The rift herald was introduced in 2016 and there is little data from 2018, so we will use a filter to extract data from 2016-2017.

Now let's say we want to work with numerical data, such as the effect of gold on the outcome of a game. With numerical data, we can obtain better statistics using regression. It's pretty clear to see that generally teams with more gold will win, because more gold means better items, which means more pressure in lane and team fights. However, there is always the occassional backdoor that turns a loss into a win, or a single team fight that turns the tides of a game. Let's clean up our dataset as we did above, and only take information that we want. Luckily for us, this dataset has a golddiff attribute that shows the difference in gold (blue team gold - red team gold) by minute. Let's extract the final minute difference for each team. The golddiff attribute is considered a column of strings, so we will use basic python functions to convert the string into a list, then take the final value. Note that a positive value means that the blue team had more gold, while a negative value means that the red team had more.

Since we also don't care which team won, let's change the dataset so that if the blue team won with a positive gold disparity, then the gold disparity stays positive. If the red team won and there was a negative gold disparity, we will change the gold disparity to positive. However, if the winning team did not have more gold, the gold disparity will be negative. In this way, we won't need the bResult and rResult columns.

In [46]:
